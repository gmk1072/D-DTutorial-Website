Ability Scores

Step by Step Creating a D&D 5e Character Tutorial

Ability scores are determined by rolling 4d6 and choosing the best three scores of those 4 dice. You have to do this for each of the 6 stats: Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma. The rolls you get each have an ability modifier that correspond, this number gets added to certain things like any ability/skill check, when attacking/doing damage, your AC, your health and other various things.

Abilities

  • Strength: Used for most melee combat and for equipping heavy armor. Melee or tough fighter-like characters would use this. Skills that use this: Athletics.
  • Dexterity:Used for most ranged combat and adds to light and medium armor class. Agile characters would use this. Skills that use this: Acrobatics, Sleight of Hand, and Stealth.
  • Constitution:Used for health pool and common durability. Anyone can make use of this but anyone that plans on getting hit would use this. No skills use this
  • Intelligence:Used for some spell casting. Some mage-like characters or anyone who is meant to be smart would use this. Skills that use this: Arcana, History, Investigation, Nature, and Religion.
  • Wisdom:Used for some spell casting. Affects passive Wisdom(perception). Older, religious, or aware characters would use this. Skills that use this: Animal Handling, Insight, Medicine, Perception, and Survival.
  • Charisma:Used for some spell casting. Charming, handsome, sly, and persuasive characters would use this. Skills that use this: Deception, Intimidation, Performance, and Persuasion.

I would not assign any stats until you have determined your class AND your race. The racial passives may allow you to place some stats in other locations for a more well rounded build. The class you choose will determine which stats should be your highest. For example as a rogue, you may wish to consider placing your highest stat in dexterity due to the rogue probably using medium armor, stealth, and finesse weapons
